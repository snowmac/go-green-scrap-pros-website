You priced everything to sell. You sat in the heat for eight hours. You made some cash. And now it's 2 PM on a Sunday and your driveway still looks like a yard sale — because it is. The last thing you want to do is drag everything back inside.

Here's how to handle garage sale leftovers efficiently, without putting unsold items back in the garage to live for another year.

## First: Resist the Urge to Take It Back In

This is the most important rule of post-garage-sale cleanup. Once something left the house for the sale, it should not re-enter the house. That box of books you couldn't sell for fifty cents and the wobbling end table that got zero offers — those are done. Making exceptions is how garages stay cluttered for years.

Give yourself an hour after the sale ends to make decisions. After that, everything on the driveway is either getting donated, listed online, or hauled away.

## Option 1: Donate Everything That's Left

This is often the fastest and most satisfying outcome. Load what's left into your car and make a run to a donation center — or schedule a pickup.

**Denver metro donation options:**
- **ARC Thrift Stores** — Colorado's large thrift network. Drop-off available at many Front Range locations; free furniture pickup by appointment (schedule in advance — same-day isn't typical)
- **Goodwill of Colorado** — Accepts most household items and clothing. Drive-through drop-off at most locations
- **Salvation Army** — Accepts clothing and household goods; furniture pickup scheduling varies
- **Local churches and community organizations** — Some accept donations directly and can move items faster than national chains

The advantage of same-day drop-off over a pickup appointment: you close the loop today. Load it, drive it, done.

### What donation centers won't take

Not everything from a garage sale is donateable, even if it's technically functional:

- Items with missing parts or safety recalls
- Car seats (due to unknown accident history)
- Mattresses (most centers won't accept them — see our [mattress removal](/mattress-removal/) page)
- Severely worn clothing or textiles
- Broken electronics or appliances that don't power on

For anything that doesn't make the donation cut, you have two remaining options.

## Option 2: Keep Selling Online

If you had high-value items that didn't sell at garage sale prices, don't donate them for fifty cents — list them online.

- **Facebook Marketplace** — the most active local platform for furniture, tools, and household items in the Denver metro. Buyers expect to pick up.
- **OfferUp** — similar audience, good for mid-range items
- **Craigslist Denver** — still active for furniture and tools

Price for quick sale, not for what you wish you'd gotten. Anything still sitting after a week gets donated or hauled.

## What About Scrap Metal?

Garage sales frequently surface items with real scrap value that didn't sell because buyers didn't recognize it:

- Old tools (wrenches, hand tools with metal components)
- Metal shelving, brackets, or hardware
- Copper pipe or wiring
- Old power equipment with metal parts
