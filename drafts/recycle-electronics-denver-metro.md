Colorado has banned the disposal of most electronics in landfills since 2013. Under the Electronic Recycling Jobs Act (SB 12-133), items like televisions, computers, monitors, printers, laptops, and video game consoles cannot go in the trash or regular recycling. Waste haulers are prohibited from knowingly collecting them.

If you're sitting on a pile of old electronics and wondering what to do with them, this guide covers every practical option in the Denver metro area — from free drop-offs to scheduled pickup services.

## Why Electronics Need Special Handling

Consumer electronics contain materials that are genuinely hazardous when they end up in a landfill: lead (especially in older CRT monitors and televisions), mercury in some backlit displays, cadmium in batteries, and various heavy metals in circuit boards. These materials can leach into soil and groundwater.

On the flip side, electronics contain valuable recoverable materials: copper, aluminum, gold, silver, and rare earth elements. Certified e-waste recyclers recover these materials and keep them in productive use.

## Free and Low-Cost Drop-Off Options

### Blue Star Recyclers (Denver / Colorado Springs)

Blue Star Recyclers is a nonprofit e-waste recycler with a location in Denver's Sun Valley neighborhood and additional sites in Colorado Springs and Boulder. They accept TVs, computers, monitors, and most consumer electronics.

Denver County residents can request a free **E-cycle Coupon** through Denver Solid Waste Management's online form, which provides a discounted rate for drop-off at Blue Star. The coupon is limited to one per household per year and is valid for Denver County residents only. Codes are typically emailed within 24 hours.

### Eco-Cycle CHaRM — Boulder

Eco-Cycle's Center for Hard-to-Recycle Materials (CHaRM) at 6400 Arapahoe Road in Boulder accepts computers, laptops, phones, chargers, and televisions. This is a strong option for residents in the Boulder/Longmont corridor.

### Sustainability Recycling — Arvada, Westminster, and Broomfield

Sustainability Recycling operates locations in Arvada, Westminster, and Broomfield and accepts televisions and monitors (flat-screen and CRT) for a per-pound fee. They accept most consumer electronics for free. CRT televisions are accepted with quantity limits per visit.

### Best Buy

Best Buy accepts most consumer electronics for recycling. TVs up to 32 inches are accepted free; larger flat screens and all CRT televisions carry a recycling fee. Limit is three items per household per day. This is one of the most convenient options given Best Buy's multiple metro locations.

### Arapahoe County Hazardous Waste Facility

Located at 7600 S. Peoria Street in Englewood, the Arapahoe County facility accepts electronics free for county residents. Available on scheduled event days — check Arapahoe County's website for current dates.

### METech Recycling — Denver

METech Recycling at 500 W 53rd Place in Denver is primarily oriented toward business and bulk recycling but accepts residential drop-offs and offers certified data destruction services for devices containing sensitive personal information.

## What Electronics Can Be Recycled

Items covered by Colorado's e-waste ban and accepted at most certified recyclers:

- Televisions (all types: CRT, LCD, LED, plasma)
- Desktop and laptop computers
- Computer monitors
- Printers and scanners
- DVD players and VCRs
- Video game consoles
- Smartphones and tablets
- Stereos and audio equipment

Items generally **not** covered (check locally):
- Large appliances (refrigerators, washers/dryers — separate recycling stream)
- Air conditioners (require Freon recovery)
- Standard household batteries (specific drop-off programs)
- Fluorescent bulbs (hazardous waste events)

## Before You Drop Off: Data Security

Wipe personal data from phones, laptops, tablets, and computers before dropping them off at any recycling location. Simply deleting files is not sufficient — reinstall the operating system to factory settings, or use a dedicated data wiping tool. For business-grade data security needs, use a certified recycler like METech that offers documented data destruction.
