October and November are the most underrated months for a home cleanout. The weather in Denver is still workable, the holiday crush hasn't arrived yet, and — critically — the tax year is still open. Donations made before December 31 count toward the current year's deduction if you itemize on your federal taxes. That's a real financial incentive to act now rather than waiting until January.

Here's how to approach a year-end cleanout strategically.

## The Tax Angle: What You Need to Know

If you itemize deductions on your federal tax return, donated goods to qualified 501(c)(3) organizations are generally deductible at fair market value. This applies to clothing, furniture, appliances, housewares, electronics, and other household goods in good usable condition.

A few key points:

- **The IRS requires "good condition or better"** for donated items to qualify. Broken, heavily worn, or non-functional items typically don't count.
- **Get a receipt for every donation.** Donation centers like Goodwill of Colorado and ARC Thrift Stores provide receipts at the time of donation. Keep these for your records.
- **You determine fair market value.** The charity can't tell you what your items are worth — that's your responsibility. Goodwill's published value guide can help as a reference point.
- **For total noncash donations over $500**, you'll need to complete IRS Form 8283.
- **For donations valued over $5,000**, a qualified appraisal is generally required.

Consult your tax advisor for guidance specific to your situation. The rules around noncash charitable contributions have nuances that vary based on your overall tax picture.

## Denver-Area Organizations That Accept Year-End Donations

Many donation centers in the metro see a surge in drop-offs in December. If you want your items processed in the current tax year, earlier is better — late December backlogs can delay receipt issuance.

**Goodwill of Colorado** — Accepts clothing, housewares, electronics, and furniture at multiple Front Range locations. Drive-through drop-off available.

**ARC Thrift Stores** — Colorado's large local thrift network, supporting people with disabilities. Accepts most household goods and offers free furniture pickup along the Front Range (schedule in advance — October and November fill up).

**Habitat for Humanity ReStore** — Focuses on furniture, appliances, and building materials. Particularly good for solid-wood furniture, tools, and home improvement items.

**Salvation Army** — Clothing, furniture, and household goods. Pickup scheduling available.

**Local shelters and community organizations** — Denver Rescue Mission and similar organizations often have specific needs for clothing, linens, and kitchenware. Call ahead to ask what they're accepting.

## What to Declutter Before Year-End

### Clothing and Textiles

The classic year-end cleanout starts in the closet. Apply the one-year rule: if you haven't worn it since last October, it goes. Bag and donate clothing, coats (especially useful for winter donation needs), bedding, and towels.

### Furniture

Furniture you've been meaning to replace, guest room pieces that go unused, and anything that's taking up space in a basement or garage is fair game. Year-end is a good time because movers are less booked, donation centers are actively accepting furniture, and you'll enter the new year with cleared space.

### Electronics

Electronics require separate handling from standard donations. Colorado has e-waste regulations — televisions, computers, monitors, and other electronics generally can't be landfilled in the regular waste stream. Many organizations accept working electronics. Non-working electronics go to certified e-waste recyclers.

See our [TV recycling](/tv-recycling/) page if you have screens that need to be disposed of responsibly.

### Appliances and Large Items

Working small appliances (blenders, toasters, coffee makers) can be donated. Large appliances with refrigerant — refrigerators, air conditioners, dehumidifiers — require proper refrigerant recovery before disposal and are typically not accepted by donation centers.

For large items you can't move yourself, a professional pickup is the practical option.

## What Can't Be Donated (and Still Needs to Go)

Not everything from a cleanout qualifies for donation, even if it's technically still intact:

- Mattresses (most organizations won't accept them)
- Items with pest concerns
- Broken or non-functional furniture and appliances
- Heavily stained textiles
- Items missing key components

## A Simple Year-End Timeline

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| October–November | Sort every room; set aside donate vs. haul |
| Early November | Schedule donation pickups (ARC furniture, etc.) |
| Mid-November | Drop off smaller donations, get receipts |
| Late November | Schedule junk removal for remainder |
| December 31 | Deadline for current-year deductible donations |

Don't leave this until the week after Thanksgiving. Donation centers get extremely busy in December, and pickup schedules for furniture fill up weeks out.
