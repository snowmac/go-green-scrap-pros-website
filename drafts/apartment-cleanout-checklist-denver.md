Moving out of a Denver apartment is stressful enough without getting hit with unexpected fees for items left behind or a space that wasn't properly cleared. A systematic cleanout approach protects your security deposit, keeps you on schedule, and avoids the scramble of trying to move furniture and trash at the last minute.

Here's a practical checklist built for Denver metro renters.

## Before You Start: Know Your Lease

Your lease spells out exactly what "move-out ready" means for your specific building. Before anything else:

- Re-read the move-out section of your lease
- Note any specific cleaning standards required
- Find out if your landlord requires professional cleaning (some do)
- Ask about a pre-move-out inspection — many Denver property managers will do a walkthrough to flag issues before your final move-out date

Addressing issues before you hand over the keys is always cheaper than having fees deducted from your deposit after the fact.

## Room-by-Room Cleanout Checklist

### Kitchen

- Remove all food from cabinets, the refrigerator, and freezer
- Clean inside and outside of all appliances (oven, microwave, refrigerator)
- Wipe down all cabinet interiors and exteriors
- Clean countertops, backsplash, and sink
- Dispose of cleaning products you're leaving behind (don't just leave them under the sink)
- Check for items stored above cabinets

### Bathrooms

- Clear all personal items from medicine cabinets, under-sink storage, and shower
- Properly dispose of expired medications (Denver-area Walgreens locations and several police stations have safe medication drop boxes)
- Clean fixtures, mirrors, and tile
- Remove any shelving or hooks you added (patch if required by lease)

### Bedrooms and Living Areas

- Remove all furniture you're not taking
- Check under beds, behind doors, and inside closets — every corner
- Remove any wall hangings and address nail holes (small holes are typically normal wear and tear in Colorado; large or excessive holes may be charged)
- Vacuum carpets or sweep hard floors
- Wipe down window sills and blinds

### Storage, Patios, and Parking

- Clear your assigned storage unit or cage completely
- Remove everything from your balcony or patio — furniture, planters, mats
- Check your parking space for items stored under cars or in corner spots
- Return any assigned keys, fobs, or garage openers

## The Big Items: Furniture and Appliances

This is where move-out cleanouts get complicated. You have a few options for furniture and large items you're not taking:

**Option 1: Donate early.**
If your furniture is in good condition, list it on Facebook Marketplace or contact ARC Thrift (which offers free furniture pickup in Colorado) at least a week before your move-out date. Don't count on donation pickups happening on short notice.

**Option 2: Sell it.**
Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace move local furniture quickly if you price it right. List early and be flexible on timing.

**Option 3: Schedule professional haul-away.**
If you're running out of time or the items aren't sellable, a junk removal team can clear furniture, appliances, and accumulated items quickly. We regularly handle apartment cleanouts for residents moving out of buildings across Thornton, Arvada, Westminster, and Denver proper.

One thing to avoid: leaving furniture in common areas or dumpster areas without permission. Most Denver apartment complexes will charge you for removing items left in unauthorized areas, and it can complicate your move-out settlement.

## What Goes in the Regular Trash vs. What Doesn't

Items you can put in your apartment's dumpster:

- Bagged household trash
- Small broken items and miscellaneous debris

Items that require special handling:

- Electronics (e-waste) — Colorado has drop-off sites; don't trash TVs or computers
- Batteries — many Denver-area hardware stores accept battery recycling
- Medications — use designated drop boxes, not the toilet or trash
- Paint — Colorado has PaintCare drop-off locations throughout the metro area

## Timing: Work Backward from Your Move-Out Date

A suggested timeline:

- **2+ weeks out:** List furniture and large items for sale or donation pickup
- **1 week out:** Schedule junk removal for anything not sold or donated
- **2–3 days out:** Deep clean each room after items are removed
- **Move-out day:** Final sweep, photos of every room, return keys

Taking photos of the apartment after you've cleared and cleaned it is one of the smartest things you can do. Timestamped photos protect you if disputes arise about the condition you left the unit in.
