"Free junk removal" gets searched a lot — and for good reason. Nobody wants to pay to get rid of stuff they don't want. The good news is that some categories of junk removal genuinely are free. The less-good news is that many things people expect to be free actually cost something, whether in money, time, or effort. Here's an honest breakdown.

## What Is Actually Free

### Scrap Metal Pickup

This is the most genuinely free category. If you have scrap metal — old appliances, copper pipe, aluminum siding, steel shelving, a broken washing machine — junk removal companies that handle scrap will pick it up at no charge. The business model works because they take the metal to a scrap yard and receive payment for the materials.

The key word is **metal**. The item needs to have meaningful metal content. A fully functional washing machine is free. A washing machine stuffed inside bags of household trash is not.

### City Bulk Pickup Programs

Denver and many surrounding municipalities offer scheduled bulk item pickup for residents. In Denver, you can request this through the 311 system. Some municipalities offer this service free of charge; others charge a per-item fee. Wait times range from a few days to a few weeks depending on availability.

The limitation: you do the work of getting items to the curb, and you work on the city's schedule, not yours.

### Donation Pickup (Working Items Only)

Some charities offer free furniture pickup for items in good condition. ARC Thrift Stores and Habitat for Humanity ReStore occasionally do pickup runs, though this varies by availability and item condition. Facebook Marketplace and Nextdoor can also result in someone picking up a working item directly from your home at no cost.

Again, condition is everything. Donation centers reject far more than they accept.

## What Costs Money (And Why)

### Mattresses

Mattresses almost always cost something to dispose of — whether that's a city pickup fee, a drop-off fee at a recycling facility, or a professional removal fee. They're bulky, difficult to handle, and most charities won't accept them. GGSP's flat-rate [mattress removal](/mattress-removal/) is $80, which covers all the handling so you don't have to deal with it.

### Refrigerators and Appliances with Refrigerant

Old fridges, freezers, AC units, and dehumidifiers contain refrigerant (Freon or modern equivalents) that must be properly recovered before recycling. This requires specialized equipment and adds cost. A bare scrap metal pickup company won't take them — you need someone equipped to handle the refrigerant.

### Couches and Upholstered Furniture

Couches don't have meaningful scrap metal value, so there's no free-pickup business model for them. They take up significant truck space, are heavy, and often end up in landfill because they're not recyclable. Professional removal is the realistic option for most couches that can't be donated. See our [couch removal page](/couch-removal/) if you're in the Denver metro.

### Mixed Loads of Junk

A mixed load — furniture, boxes of miscellaneous junk, bags of trash, broken items — requires labor, truck space, and disposal fees at a transfer station or landfill. This costs money, and that's legitimate. Anyone offering to remove a full truckload of mixed junk for free is either cutting environmental corners or adding hidden fees somewhere else.

### TVs and Electronics

Colorado law bans most electronics from landfills (SB 12-133). E-waste disposal requires certified recyclers, and that processing costs money. Some retailers offer free recycling for smaller items; larger or older TVs typically carry a fee.

## The Gray Zone: "Free" Scrap with a Catch

Sometimes you'll see ads for free junk removal on Craigslist or community boards. A few things to watch for:

- **Cherry-picking** — the "free" pickup only takes the valuable scrap metal and leaves everything else
- **Illegal dumping** — no verified information about where the load ends up
- **Hidden trip charges** — quoted as free, then a "fuel surcharge" or "disposal fee" added on arrival

Working with a local, established business gives you accountability that a random Craigslist ad doesn't.
