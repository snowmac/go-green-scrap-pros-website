Getting rid of an old refrigerator sounds straightforward until you realize it can't go in the trash, most junk removal companies won't take it without special handling, and the Freon inside it is subject to federal environmental regulations. That said, there are several practical options for Colorado homeowners — and one of them might actually put money back in your pocket.

## Why Refrigerator Disposal Is Complicated

Old refrigerators and freezers contain refrigerant (commonly called Freon, though modern units use other compounds) that must be properly recovered before the appliance is scrapped or landfilled. Under U.S. EPA regulations (Section 608 of the Clean Air Act), the refrigerant in a disposed appliance must be recovered using certified equipment — it cannot simply be vented to the atmosphere.

This matters for a few practical reasons:

- Most municipal landfills and transfer stations won't accept refrigerators with refrigerant still inside
- Scrap yards that accept appliances are responsible for ensuring Freon has been properly recovered
- Cutting refrigerant lines yourself or allowing refrigerant to vent is illegal under federal law and harms the ozone layer
- Reputable junk removal companies that handle appliances have the equipment and trained personnel to manage refrigerant correctly

The bottom line: work with a legitimate service, not someone who offers a too-good-to-be-true "free pickup" with no explanation of how they handle the refrigerant.

## Your Options for Refrigerator Disposal in Colorado

### Option 1: Xcel Energy Appliance Recycling Rebate

If your old refrigerator or freezer is still operational and you're an Xcel Energy customer, you may qualify for a **$50 rebate** when you recycle it through their appliance turn-in program. Xcel coordinates pickup and ensures the refrigerant is properly recovered and the appliance is recycled. This is one of the best deals available for Denver-area homeowners replacing a working but inefficient fridge.

Eligibility requirements apply — the unit typically needs to be in working condition and meet certain size and age criteria. Contact Xcel Energy directly to confirm current program details.

### Option 2: Denver City Appliance Collection (Free)

Denver residents serviced by Solid Waste Management can schedule a **free appliance collection** through the city. The City of Denver handles these pickups specifically to ensure metals are recycled and Freon is handled safely. Call Solid Waste Management at (720) 913-1311 to schedule. This option works well but has typical municipal wait times of one to two weeks.

### Option 3: Appliance Retailer Haul-Away

When purchasing a new refrigerator from a major retailer — Home Depot, Lowe's, Best Buy — most offer haul-away of the old unit for a fee. This is convenient if you're doing a direct swap, but won't work if you're just clearing out a second fridge from the garage without buying a replacement.

### Option 4: Professional Junk Removal

For second refrigerators in garages, basement units, or old chest freezers that aren't connected to a new appliance purchase, a professional junk removal service is often the most practical choice. A reputable company will handle the refrigerant correctly and get the appliance out of your home the same day or next day.

## The Recycling Story: What Happens to the Materials

Refrigerators are actually excellent candidates for recycling. A typical unit contains:

- **Steel cabinet and frame** — high-value ferrous scrap
- **Aluminum shelves and interior components**
- **Copper tubing** in the refrigerant lines and compressor
- **Refrigerant** — recovered and processed by certified reclaimers
- **Foam insulation** — not always recyclable, but increasingly processed by specialized facilities

Almost all the metal content of a refrigerator can be recovered and returned to the supply chain, which is why reputable appliance recyclers often value them over straight-to-landfill disposal.

## Tips Before the Pickup

- **Empty and defrost the unit** completely before pickup day
- **Disconnect power** at least 24 hours before the scheduled removal
- **Remove any food** — this sounds obvious, but easy to forget with a garage chest freezer
- **Keep doors closed** until pickup (this helps prevent child entrapment and reduces refrigerant off-gassing)
- **Check for any items stored on top or around the unit** — give your removal crew clear access
