That treadmill in the basement was a great idea at the time. Now it's a very expensive clothes rack taking up half the room. If you're ready to reclaim that space, you have a few solid options — but home gym equipment comes with its own removal challenges that are worth understanding before you start.

## Why Home Gym Equipment Is Hard to Move

Treadmills, ellipticals, weight machines, and home gyms are some of the most awkward items to remove from a house. A mid-range treadmill typically weighs 200–300 lbs, often lives in a basement or second-floor room, and is too bulky for a standard doorway without partial disassembly. Weight racks, cable machines, and multi-station home gyms can be heavier still and require tools to break down.

If you've ever tried to wrestle a treadmill up a flight of stairs solo, you already know the appeal of calling in help.

## Option 1: Donate It (If It Still Works)

Working exercise equipment is genuinely in demand. Before you schedule a haul-away, consider whether your gear could go to a new home:

- **Habitat for Humanity ReStores** in the Denver metro occasionally accept exercise equipment — call ahead to confirm.
- **Local gyms and community centers** in Thornton, Arvada, Westminster, and surrounding areas sometimes accept donated equipment for member use.
- **Facebook Marketplace / Nextdoor** — a free or low-cost listing for working equipment moves fast. Buyers typically handle pickup themselves.
- **ARC Thrift** (Colorado-based) will pick up furniture and large items in good condition at no charge along the Front Range — check their website for current acceptance policies.

The key qualifier: the equipment needs to be in working condition. A treadmill with a burned-out motor, a broken belt, or missing parts won't be accepted by most donation programs.

## Option 2: Sell It

If your equipment is in decent shape, you may be able to recover some of what you paid. Used treadmills, rowing machines, and free weights sell regularly on:

- **Facebook Marketplace** (most active for bulky local items)
- **Craigslist Denver**
- **OfferUp**

Price realistically — used gym equipment depreciates steeply. A treadmill that cost $1,500 new might fetch $150–$400 depending on brand, age, and condition. Offer local pickup only and be prepared for buyers who want to negotiate.

## Option 3: Scrap the Metal

Many home gym components — particularly free weights, metal frames, and weight plates — have real scrap value as steel or iron. If the equipment isn't in sellable condition, stripping it down and taking the metal to a scrap yard is a legitimate option. Cast iron weights in particular are worth something at current metal prices.
