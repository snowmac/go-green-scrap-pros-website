Most people who call a junk removal company just want the stuff gone. That's completely fair. But if you've ever wondered what actually happens to your old couch, broken appliances, or garage full of metal — here's the honest answer of how we handle it.

## It Doesn't All Go to the Landfill

This is the biggest misconception about junk removal. A responsible hauler isn't just driving a glorified garbage truck. The sorting that happens after pickup — and sometimes on-site — determines where each item ends up, and a lot of it never touches a landfill.

Here's how we break it down.

## Step 1: On-Site Sorting

Before anything goes on the truck, we assess what you've got. Items that are clearly in good condition get separated from items headed for scrap or disposal. This matters because:

- Donateable items need to arrive at donation centers in usable shape
- Scrap metal needs to be separated by type for recycling facilities
- Electronics require their own handling stream
- Appliances with refrigerant have to be flagged for proper recovery

Doing this sorting at the source — your property — is more efficient than sorting at a transfer station. It also ensures that items with value don't get accidentally disposed of.

## Step 2: Donation

Furniture, housewares, clothing, working electronics, tools — anything in genuinely reusable condition gets routed to local donation partners rather than disposed of. In the Denver metro area, that includes organizations like Goodwill of Colorado, ARC Thrift Stores, and Habitat for Humanity ReStore.

When we bring items to a donation center, someone who can't afford to buy new gets access to something useful. That's a better outcome for everyone.

One important note: donation centers have standards. Stained, broken, or unsafe items don't get donated — they'd just be discarding them from the other end. We're honest about what can and can't be donated, and we don't make promises we can't keep.

## Step 3: Scrap Metal Recycling

Metal is one of the most valuable things in a typical junk haul. Old appliances, shelving, metal furniture frames, pipes, wire, tools — all of this has real recycling value.

We separate ferrous metals (steel and iron) from non-ferrous (aluminum, copper, brass) because they're processed differently and have different values at recycling facilities. Metal recycled this way re-enters the manufacturing stream and becomes new products. It's genuinely circular — not greenwashing.

This is also why we offer [free scrap metal pickup](/scrap-metal-pickup/) for loads that are primarily metal. The recycling value offsets the cost of the pickup.

## Step 4: Electronics and E-Waste

Colorado has regulations around electronics disposal — TVs, computers, monitors, and other electronics can't legally be landfilled in the same stream as regular trash. We route e-waste to certified Colorado e-waste recyclers, where components like circuit boards, glass, and metals are processed by facilities equipped to handle them safely.

If you're getting rid of a TV as part of a larger cleanout, it goes into this separate stream. See our [TV recycling](/tv-recycling/) page for details on how we handle screens specifically.

## Step 5: Appliance Processing

Refrigerators, air conditioners, freezers, and dehumidifiers contain refrigerant that must be recovered by certified technicians before the appliance can be scrapped or discarded. We ensure this step is handled properly — the refrigerant is recovered, the metal is recycled, and documentation follows the appliance through the chain.

Washing machines and dryers don't contain refrigerant, so they go directly into the scrap metal stream.

## Step 6: What Actually Goes to the Landfill

We won't pretend everything can be recycled. Some materials genuinely have no recycling pathway — heavily damaged items, certain composite materials, contaminated materials. These get disposed of at licensed facilities serving the Denver metro area.

Our goal is to minimize this stream, not eliminate it entirely. Honest landfill diversion is better than exaggerated claims.

## Why This Matters

Choosing an eco-conscious hauler over a general trash service isn't just feel-good — it has real effects:

- Less material in Colorado landfills
- Usable goods reach people who need them
- Recyclable metal stays in the manufacturing loop
- Hazardous components (refrigerant, e-waste) are handled safely

When you call Go Green Scrap Pros, you're not just clearing space. You're choosing a disposal chain that treats your stuff with more care than a dumpster would.
